Act I
June 2023 · RGIPT

The Last Summer

The summer in RGIPT was blistering, monotonous, and seemingly never-ending. I was acutely aware that this was my last summer in college. But little did I know— this final summer would mark the beginning of something unforgettable.

"I had never experienced isolation in such a beautiful way."

I was set to leave for CSIR-IIP—an industrial research organization nestled at the edge of Rajaji National Park. Where the vast expanse of wilderness seamlessly blended with the city's edges. Towering mountains loomed in the distance, and dense greenery exhaled a quiet serenity.

Act II

Deep in the Forest

The hostel stood deep inside the forest—a kilometre from the lab. At first, the remoteness seemed daunting, but soon I began to revel in it. The distance gave me time to reflect. To grow.

I made new friends. Cultivated new hobbies. And began writing my thoughts on a swinging jhoola near my room. Every evening, I watched the sunset— pink-tinged clouds drifting lazily, a lone bird perched atop the highest branch.

"They weren't just images; they were feelings."
I even started listening to ghazals.
Act III
23rd June · Guitar Class

A Girl in Yellow

One of my longtime passions had been music, so I decided to join a guitar class. My guitar had been lying untouched since college—it felt like the perfect time to reconnect.

I cycled 1.5 kilometres, following Google Maps. Asked about the fees and schedule. Rewarded myself with a plate of the famous steamed momos from a nearby stall.

Amidst all this, I noticed someone— a girl in a yellow dress— but I looked away, unwilling to invite unnecessary distractions into my peaceful world.
Act IV

The First Class

I still remember my first class vividly—how I was asked about my knowledge of music, my hobbies, my dedication. But what stood out most wasn't the lesson.

It was her. The girl in yellow from before, now dressed in a red t-shirt. She was beautiful, yet in a way that wasn't intimidating. Her innocence made her glow, and her laughter filled the room with light.

"Her red cheeks deepened like rose petals rubbed between fingers. She played the guitar with childlike enthusiasm, her joy contagious."

Though I was introduced to everyone in the class, I couldn't muster the courage to ask her name. Instead, I stole glances—subtle, fleeting.

Act V

Three Months of Silence

This became my routine for three months. Lab by 9:30 AM. Guitar class by 5:45 PM. And the terrifying ride back— pitch black and lonely, pedaling fast, haunted by stories of tigers, elephants, and snakes narrated by the hostel guard.

Earphones in. Ghazals playing. Eyes admiring the clouds.

"For the first time in years, I wasn't chasing anything or anyone.
I was simply living."
Act VI
August · 15 Days of Absence

The Strange Void

Then August arrived. For fifteen days, she stopped coming to class. I felt a strange void, a boredom I couldn't explain. Maybe I had grown used to her presence.

But then, she returned. And suddenly, the light was back.

I wanted to talk to her, but hesitation held me back. I had no excuse. No pretext. Days passed. The guitar strings hummed. The sunsets painted their stories. And still—silence.

Act VII
4th September · A Small Gesture

The Beginning

On the 4th of September, I did the simplest thing— I sent a meme about guitars.

She reacted to it. I assumed that was it.

But it wasn't.

Then—unexpectedly—the next morning: she sent me a meme.
A small gesture. But to me, it was everything.

🎸 *meme about guitar chords*
haha 😄 *another guitar meme*
so... do you write too?
yes — poetry, mostly. want to read?
Act VIII
8th September · Cloud End Point

A Thousand Feet Above

I visited George Everest—a place 1,000 feet above sea level. I stood at Cloud End Point, feeling the mist against my skin, inhaling the cold, crisp air.

It was a good day. And the next morning, I found a message in my inbox.

Our conversation started casually—centered around guitar classes. A few messages. Nothing extraordinary on the surface.

But something was shifting.

Epilogue
16th September · The Last Train

I'm Leaving. Hope Our Paths Cross Again.

As I boarded my train back home on the 16th of September, I sent her one last message:

"I'm leaving. Hope our paths cross again."

Maybe it was fate, or maybe it was that message—but something changed. Even after leaving, we kept talking. We shared more. About music, literature, poetry.

I sent her paradoxical thoughts, memes about ghazals. She responded with her writings—beautiful, imaginative, deep. I was awestruck by her mind, her creativity.

And in those words, I found a connection I never expected.

What started as fleeting glances
turned into something
Real. Lasting.

And so, our conversations continue—
till today, and hopefully, ♡ forever.
